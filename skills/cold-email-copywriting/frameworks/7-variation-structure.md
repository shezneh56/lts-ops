# 7-Variation Framework

Each cold email campaign includes **7 unique Step 1 variations** that approach the same value proposition from different angles. This ensures:
- A/B testing across messaging styles
- Natural variation for deliverability
- Multiple entry points for different buyer mindsets

---

## Variation 1: Journey/Process Angle

**Purpose:** Focus on the prospect's customer/user journey and friction points.

**Hook pattern:**
> What if [audience] at {COMPANY} could [complete desired action] without [friction/obstacle]?

**Example:**
```
{FIRST_NAME}, what if {prospective students|future students|incoming students} at {COMPANY} could find their program and enroll without {getting lost in your site|struggling with navigation|hitting dead ends}?
```

**Best for:** Enrollment, onboarding, conversion-focused messaging

---

## Variation 2: Search/Navigation Angle

**Purpose:** Address discoverability and findability problems.

**Hook pattern:**
> Can [audience] at {COMPANY} actually [find/discover/navigate to] [what they need]?

**Example:**
```
{FIRST_NAME}, can students at {COMPANY} {actually find what they're looking for|easily discover programs|navigate to the right information} on your website?
```

**Best for:** Complex sites, large catalogs, multi-product portfolios

---

## Variation 3: Accessibility/Compliance Angle

**Purpose:** Lead with compliance concerns (ADA, WCAG, regulations).

**Hook pattern:**
> How confident is {COMPANY} in your [compliance area]?

**Example:**
```
{FIRST_NAME}, how confident is {COMPANY} in your website's {accessibility compliance|accessibility standards|ADA compliance}?
```

**Best for:** Higher ed, healthcare, government, financial services

---

## Variation 4: Custom/Industry-Specific Angle

**Purpose:** Tailor to the prospect's unique context.

**Possible angles:**
- Multi-location/multi-campus challenges
- Industry-specific pain points
- Seasonal/cyclical business challenges
- Recent company news or changes

**Example (multi-campus):**
```
{FIRST_NAME}, {COMPANY} serves students across {6 locations|multiple campuses|central Nebraska}. {Does your website feel unified - or fragmented?|Is the experience consistent everywhere?}
```

**Best for:** When you have specific intel on the prospect

---

## Variation 5: Credibility Angle

**Purpose:** Lead with awards, recognition, or partnership longevity.

**Hook pattern:**
> We [achievement/recognition] for our [relevant work].

**Example:**
```
{FIRST_NAME}, we {just won|recently received|were awarded} Optimizely's 2024 Partner of the Year {award|recognition} for our higher ed work.
```

**Best for:** When you have strong proof points to lead with

---

## Variation 6: Problem-Focused Angle

**Purpose:** Name the pain directly—drop-off, friction, lost conversions.

**Hook pattern:**
> How many [audience] give up on {COMPANY}'s [asset] before [desired outcome]?

**Example:**
```
{FIRST_NAME}, how many {prospective students|future Raiders|interested students} give up on {COMPANY}'s website before they {find what they need|complete enrollment|discover the right program}?
```

**Best for:** Prospects likely experiencing measurable pain

---

## Variation 7: Direct Value Question

**Purpose:** Challenge whether their current asset is helping or hurting.

**Hook pattern:**
> Is {COMPANY}'s [asset] actually [helping/enabling] [audience]—or [getting in the way/creating friction]?

**Example:**
```
{FIRST_NAME}, is {COMPANY}'s website {actually helping students enroll|supporting your enrollment goals|working for you} - or {just getting in the way|creating friction|making things harder}?
```

**Best for:** Decision-makers who think strategically

---

## Step 2: Follow-Up

**Purpose:** Gentle nudge referencing original email.

**Structure:**
1. **Subject:** `Re: {original subject line with spintax}`
2. **Open:** Bump/circle back language
3. **Body:** Restate core value in new words
4. **CTA:** Specific ask (15 minutes, quick chat)

**Example:**
```
Subject: Re: {student enrollment|enrollment experience|the enrollment journey} at {COMPANY}

{FIRST_NAME}, {wanted to bump this up in your inbox|circling back on this|following up}.

We've helped a {major college district|large community college system|79,000-student district} grow from a {confusing site|frustrating website|fragmented experience} to a {streamlined enrollment machine|conversion-focused platform|site that actually works}.

If {improving how students find and choose programs|streamlining enrollment|fixing your digital experience} at {COMPANY} is on your radar, {I'd love 15 minutes|let's find time to talk|happy to share what worked}.

{SENDER_EMAIL_SIGNATURE}
```

---

## Angle Selection Tips

| Prospect Type | Best Lead Angles |
|---------------|------------------|
| C-suite | V5 (Credibility), V7 (Direct Value) |
| VP/Director | V1 (Journey), V6 (Problem) |
| Technical (CIO, IT Director) | V3 (Compliance), V2 (Navigation) |
| Industry with unique context | V4 (Custom) |
